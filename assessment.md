## Layer Assessment

### Layer 1 — Public Presence: 2/10

**Current:** Facebook page with basic info, Instagram with 47 posts. No indexed standalone website. Branded Google search does not surface a premium web presence.

**Gap:** A visitor referred by Facebook has no owned landing page to convert on. The gym is invisible to someone searching "gym Malvar" with no prior social exposure.

**Priority:** CRITICAL. This is the single highest-leverage Layer 1 intervention available.

**Recommended build:**
- Homepage with local trust stack, offers, CTA
- 10 pages covering full discovery-to-join journey
- GBP optimization with photos, services, reviews
- SEO foundation: `gym Malvar`, `fitness Batangas`, `body assessment Malvar`

---

### Layer 2 — Lead Capture: 1/10

**Current:** Inquiries appear to go through Messenger DMs or walk-in. No public booking link, no lead form, no captured email/phone outside social.

**Gap:** Every lead is manually handled. No pipeline visibility. Leakage between discovery and membership is likely significant.

**Priority:** CRITICAL. Every day without a funnel is lost data and lost revenue.

**Recommended build:**
- Free Assessment booking form → CRM intake
- Join Now multi-step form with local payment checkout
- Lead source tracking (FB, Instagram, Google, referral)
- Auto-response and follow-up sequence

---

### Layer 3 — Operations: 2/10

**Current:** Membership tracking, coach assignments, attendance, and bookings appear to be manual or informal based on available public data.

**Gap:** No member CRM = no visibility into who's at risk of churning, who needs a check-in, or which coach is overloaded.

**Priority:** HIGH. The coaching culture is there — the infrastructure to support it isn't.

**Recommended build:**
- Member CRM: name, goal, program, coach, join date, attendance, status
- Lead pipeline: New Inquiry → Joined / Lost stages
- Class booking system with capacity and waitlist
- Coach assignment and utilization tracking

---

### Layer 4 — Growth: 3/10

**Current:** Strong content cadence with good pillars. Community recognition posts. Assessment offer is growth-positive. But no review engine, referral system, or SEO infrastructure.

**Gap:** Social creates attention. Nothing systematically converts that attention into reviews, referrals, or return visits.

**Priority:** MEDIUM. Should be built in Phase 2 after funnel is live.

**Recommended build:**
- Google review request trigger (post-milestone, post-assessment)
- Referral campaign module (bring-a-friend mechanics)
- 90-day transformation challenge infrastructure
- Content calendar system with GBP posting

---

### Layer 5 — Intelligence: 1/10

**Current:** No observable analytics, reporting, or owner dashboard.

**Gap:** The owner is running blind. No data on what's working, what's leaking, who's about to quit.

**Priority:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Even basic KPI visibility changes decision quality dramatically.

**Recommended build:**
- Owner dashboard: leads, active members, at-risk, revenue, conversions
- Lead source performance
- Attendance trend tracking
- Monthly owner brief (automated)
- AI-powered next-action recommendations

## Assessment Verdict

**Iron Tribe Fitness is a strong gym with a weak digital infrastructure score — but that gap is entirely addressable.**

The business already has:
- A coherent brand with a defensible community-coaching positioning
- A more sophisticated offer than its digital presence communicates
- Content quality above most gyms at its scale
- An audience that responds to coaching, transformation, and accountability

What it doesn't have — yet — is the infrastructure to convert its brand warmth into measurable, scalable revenue. That is precisely what Aurora Digital Foundry manufactures.

**Infrastructure ROI Potential: EXTREMELY HIGH.**

The delta between a ₱35k/month gym running on DMs and a ₱150k+/month gym running on a full digital operating system is almost entirely an infrastructure gap.
